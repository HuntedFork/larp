# A Spell For The Binding of Souls

## Overview

My Daughter. Every wytch hath her own way of becoming the best wytch which she can be. Your feet need not follow the path which I have walked, but if you choose to follow in mine footsteps then I hope to have made your travel easier, that you may travel farther than I ever have. To that end, I leave with you this spell. As with all Magick, its drawbacks will outweigh its uses unless applied with utmost care. I give to you three warnings, with the hope that you will think thrice before you cast it. Firstly, NEVER use it unless you are sure of the goodwill of the spirit with which you wish to merge. Secondly, I hope I need not remind you that touching your soul to another is permanent — you cannot share that kind of space and walk away unaffected. And lastly, but most importantly, it is not a spell you can use twice. Holding one soul in you at a time is enough of a challenge; there is no sane reason to try for three.

## Components

Thou shalt gather the following before the ritual begins:

- An ensouled object — one bearing a mark such as this upon it:

  ![Soul symbol](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Smiley.svg/1280px-Smiley.svg.png)

- The corresponding envelope that matches thy object's symbol
- One ritual guide, who shall read the words aloud
- One subject, who shall receive and carry the soul henceforth
- Two witnesses, whose voices shall seal the binding

## Steps

1. **Prepare the ground.** Scratch this symbol into the earth, large enough that the subject may stand within it:

   ![Soul symbol](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Smiley.svg/1280px-Smiley.svg.png)

2. **Give the object to the subject.** They must hold it in their hands throughout the rite, that the soul within may feel the warmth of its new home.

3. **Instruct the witnesses.** They shall speak the words *"I witness"* after each stanza, that their voices may anchor the working to this world.

4. **Read aloud the incantation, pausing after each stanza for the witnesses:**

   *By blood and breath and bone made bare,*
   *We open wide this vessel here.*
   *What walks between the worlds unseen,*
   *Draw near, draw near, draw near.*

   **Witnesses: "I witness."**

   *Thou who hast wandered, lost and long,*
   *Whose name was writ in fading song —*
   *We call thee now by what thou art,*
   *Come rest within a living heart.*

   **Witnesses: "I witness."**

   *Two souls, one skin, one breath, one flame,*
   *Henceforth thou art and they the same.*
   *What we have opened, let it close.*
   *What they have taken, no one knows.*

   **Witnesses: "I witness."**

5. **Introduce the soul.** Open the envelope with care. Read only the front of the card aloud, in these words:
   *"This is [Name of entity]. Their nature is [entity traits]. Do you accept them into your soul?"*

6. **Seal the acceptance.** The subject shall remove the tag from the object whilst saying *"I accept!"* — and the witnesses shall answer *"I witness!"*

7. **Call the freeze.** Cry out *"Time freeze!"* and place the envelope into the subject's hands. They may now read what is written within, in silence, and know fully what they have taken into themselves.

8. **Release the world.** When they have finished reading, they shall cry *"Game on"* — and the rite is complete. What hath been joined cannot be sundered.

_If one person ever attempts to be the subject a second time, have them immediately see a story teller. The result will be neither good nor fun_
